Race Courses

Choose Your Challenge

Jello Salad

Distance: 150 miles
Elevation Gain: 20,192 ft
Terrain: 49% Paved, 51% Unpaved
Estimated Time: 12-40 hours

A UME classic. A challenging 150-mile route that combines world class single track with high-elevation passes and a mix of terrain. This course is a great introduction to the UME experience and a perfect stepping stone for those looking to tackle the longer routes in future years.

Highlights:
  • Scenic red rock formations
  • Technical single-track sections
  • Overnight camping or pedal through

Fry Sauce

Distance: 350+ miles
Elevation Gain: 38,000 ft
Terrain: 40% Paved, 60% Unpaved
Estimated Time: 2-5 Days

A new offering for 2026, the Fry Sauce course is a 350+ mile route that explores the heart of Utah's backcountry. This course is designed for experienced riders looking for a true adventure. Expect high-altitude mountain passes, technical single-track sections, and remote wilderness areas. This route will be best ridden on a mountain bike with some suspension, though rigid and drop bar bikes will gain an advantage later in the route.

Highlights:
  • High-altitude mountain passes (10,000+ ft)
  • Technical single-track sections
  • Remote wilderness areas

Funeral Potatoes

Distance: 730+ miles
Elevation Gain: 69,000+ ft
Terrain: 31% Paved, 69% Unpaved
Estimated Time: 5+ Days

This is the complete UME experience. A 730+ mile route that traverses the most remote and a varied terrain Utah has to offer. This course is designed for the most adventurous and experienced riders looking for an epic challenge.

Highlights:
  • Extreme elevation gain and technical descents
  • Multi-day adventure format
  • Self-supported sections available

Course Information

Terrain Types

Road: Paved county and state roads

Gravel: Dirt and gravel roads. Some maintained more that others

Single Track: Mountain bike trails and good ole fashioned chunk

Safety & Support

Please throughly plan and prepare for your chosen course. This is entirely unsupported and self-navigated. You are responsible for your own safety and logistics.

Elevation

Routes traverse elevations from 4,000 ft to 10,500 ft. Athletes advised to acclimate before the race.
