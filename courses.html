Race Courses

Choose Your Challenge

Jello Salad

Distance: 100 miles
Elevation Gain: 6,500 ft
Terrain: 40% Road, 30% Gravel, 30% Trail
Estimated Time: 7-10 hours

Perfect for cyclists looking to challenge themselves with a full day of mixed-terrain riding. This route showcases some of Utah's most beautiful landscapes while maintaining manageable technical difficulty.

Highlights:
  • Scenic red rock formations
  • Rolling mountain passes
  • Well-marked trails
  • Multiple aid stations

Fry Sauce

Distance: 200 miles
Elevation Gain: 14,000 ft
Terrain: 35% Road, 25% Gravel, 40% Trail
Estimated Time: 18-24 hours

The flagship event. A true test of endurance across two days of non-stop cycling. Navigate technical terrain and high-elevation passes while experiencing Utah's most dramatic landscapes.

Highlights:
  • High-altitude mountain passes (10,000+ ft)
  • Technical single-track sections
  • Remote wilderness areas
  • Overnight camping or pedal through

Funeral Potatoes

Distance: 300+ miles
Elevation Gain: 22,000+ ft
Terrain: 25% Road, 20% Gravel, 55% Technical Trail
Estimated Time: 35-48 hours

The ultimate test of human endurance and skill. Multi-day ultra-distance racing through some of the most challenging terrain in North America. Reserved for experienced ultra-cyclists.

Highlights:
  • Extreme elevation gain and technical descents
  • Multi-day adventure racing format
  • Self-supported sections available
  • Premium support and safety infrastructure

Course Information

Terrain Types

Road: Paved county and state roads

Gravel: Dirt and gravel roads. Some maintained more that others

Single Track: Mountain bike trails and good ole fashioned chunk

Safety & Support

Please throughly plan and prepare for your chosen course. This is entirely unsupported and self-navigated. You are responsible for your own safety and logistics.

Elevation

Routes traverse elevations from 4,000 ft to 10,500 ft. Athletes advised to acclimate before the race.
